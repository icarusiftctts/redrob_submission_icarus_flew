Labelling Criteria 

The JD explicitly warns participants not to do keyword matching and instead evaluate what the candidate actually built. That means your labeling process should not merely optimize your current heuristics—it should approximate how a senior engineering hiring panel would evaluate candidates for this role.

Below is the blueprint I would use if I wanted to maximize leaderboard performance.

Overall Goal

You're not labeling candidates.

You're estimating hidden relevance labels.

Think of every candidate as receiving a hidden score from Redrob.

Your task is to approximate that score.

Label Scale

Never use binary labels.

Use five grades.

Label	Meaning	Hidden Relevance
4	Outstanding hire	Almost certainly Top-10
3	Strong hire	Very likely Top-50
2	Acceptable	Worth interviewing
1	Weak fit	Possible but unlikely
0	Reject	Should not appear near top

This aligns much better with NDCG than yes/no labels.

Labeling Philosophy

The JD repeatedly emphasizes one principle:

Production systems > keywords.

Examples directly from the JD:

Someone who built recommendation systems may outrank someone listing Pinecone.
LangChain demos do not imply strong production ML.
Product ownership matters.
Evaluation thinking matters.
Shippers beat researchers.
Behavioral availability matters.

Therefore, your rubric must reflect this.

Active Learning Strategy

Don't label randomly.

Round 1

Label

Top 100

Round 2

Label

Candidates ranked

80–250

Round 3

Find candidates whose total score is close to promotion/demotion thresholds and label those.

These "borderline" examples are where weight optimization will have the greatest effect.

Quality Control

Every 25 candidates

Pause.

Take five random earlier candidates.

Re-label them.

If the label changes by more than one grade

Your rubric isn't stable.

Rewrite it.

Optimization

Once you have approximately 200 labeled candidates:

Do not retrain the entire model.

Instead:

Keep all handcrafted feature extractors.
Keep honeypot detection.
Keep reasoning.
Keep semantic embeddings.

Optimize only

linear feature weights
behavioral contribution
multiplier strengths

This preserves the strengths of your current architecture while making the ranking empirically calibrated.
